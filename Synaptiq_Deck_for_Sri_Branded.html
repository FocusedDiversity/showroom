We've Done This Before

Here's What We Learned

Staffing Org

Built From Scratch
Situation

No governance team, no ownership. Three systems (scheduling, billing, reporting) each had their own version of the numbers.

Outcome

Clear owners for each data product. Fill rates improved, revenue became predictable, and the constant "whose number is right?" debates stopped.

Key Lesson

Get ownership right first. Everything else builds on that.

Operations Org

Needed Quick Wins
Situation

Skeptical leadership, limited runway. The team was spending all its time fixing bad data after the fact. Rework on top of rework.

Outcome

Shifted to catching errors at the point of entry. Service outcomes improved fast enough to secure continued funding and exec buy-in.

Key Lesson

The first 90 days decide whether you get a year two.

Legal Org

Teams Misaligned
Situation

Three teams, three interpretations of the same data. Cases kept getting bounced between groups because nobody agreed on definitions.

Outcome

One governed view, shared definitions. Processing time dropped, rework disappeared, and teams stopped blaming each other's data.

Key Lesson

The hardest part is never the data. It's getting people to agree.

Same pattern every time: get the right people in place, don't over-plan, and show something real in the first 90 days. That's it.

Architecture & Governance: The Foundation Capability

Fundamentals

The policies, tools, and documents needed to manage and govern data in a modern, data-centric organization.

  • Data governance charter and vision
  • Data classification and sensitivity standards
  • Metadata management practices
  • Data catalog and business glossary
  • Roles: data owners, stewards, custodians

Process

Processes that define how data is stored, managed, collected, and secured across the enterprise.

  • Data quality rules and validation
  • Data lifecycle management
  • Access control and security protocols
  • Change management for data assets
  • Incident response for data issues

Architecture

How data flows through the organization to inform strategy for managing and governing data effectively.

  • Enterprise data architecture blueprint
  • Data flow and lineage mapping
  • Integration patterns and standards
  • Technology stack alignment
  • Scalable storage and compute strategy

Communications

A defined framework for documenting, communicating, and enforcing data governance across the organization.

  • Governance council and meeting cadence
  • Stakeholder reporting and dashboards
  • Training and enablement programs
  • Policy distribution and acknowledgment
  • Cross-functional alignment rituals

Stakeholder Discovery: How We Talk to Your Organization

1 Executive Alignment Sessions

One-on-one or small group sessions with senior leaders to understand strategic priorities, pain points, and success criteria.

  • C-suite and VP-level sponsors
  • 60-minute structured interviews
  • Focus: vision, priorities, political landscape

2 Department Workshops

Collaborative sessions with each business unit to map data flows, pain points, and high-value use cases.

  • Cross-functional teams of 6-10 people
  • 90-minute facilitated workshops

Architecture and tooling reviews with data engineering, IT, and platform teams to assess infrastructure readiness.

  • 90-minute working sessions
  • Focus: tooling, pipelines, gaps, tech debt

4 "Art of the Possible" Sessions

Forward-looking workshops to generate and prioritize use cases that demonstrate governance value.

  • Mixed business and technical attendees
  • 2-hour creative brainstorming
  • Focus: quick wins, high-impact opportunities

Prioritization Framework

Data Product Identification & Prioritization Rubric

Criterion Weight Low (1) Medium (2) High (3)
Business Impact 30% Supports a single team's workflow Affects multiple teams or a key business process Directly tied to revenue, compliance, or strategic initiative
Data Quality Pain 20% Occasional inconsistencies, workarounds exist Regular rework required, trust issues emerging Decisions delayed or wrong due to data quality
Stakeholder Readiness 20% No clear owner, low engagement Willing owner, some organizational support Executive sponsor, active steward, team aligned
Feasibility 15% Major infrastructure or process changes needed Moderate effort, existing tools partially support Can be done with current tools and team
Visibility / Quick Win 15% Results hard to demonstrate to leadership Tangible improvement within a quarter High-profile win within 30-60 days
How to use: Score each candidate data product 1-3 on each criterion, multiply by weight, and sum for a prioritized backlog. Products scoring 2.5+ are strong first-wave candidates. Re-score quarterly as context evolves.

Measuring Success

Data Governance Balanced Scorecard

◆ Data Quality & Trust

  • Data quality score (completeness, accuracy, timeliness)
  • Number of critical data issues reported vs. resolved
  • Percentage of key datasets with defined quality rules
  • Data consumer trust survey scores
  • Reduction in rework caused by data errors

◎ Adoption & Engagement

  • Percentage of data products with assigned stewards
  • Active users of data catalog / glossary
  • Number of departments participating in governance
  • Training completion rates across roles
  • Governance council meeting attendance and action closure

★ Operational Efficiency

  • Time to resolve data access requests
  • Reduction in duplicated datasets and reports
  • Percentage of data pipelines with lineage documented
  • Mean time to detect and fix data incidents
  • Reduction in manual data preparation hours

▸ Business Value & Compliance

  • Revenue or cost impact of governed data products
  • Regulatory audit readiness score
  • Number of data-driven decisions enabled per quarter
  • Time to deliver new analytics or reports
  • Risk reduction: policy violations and breach exposure
Establish baselines in Phase 1, set targets in Phase 2, report monthly from Phase 3 onward
